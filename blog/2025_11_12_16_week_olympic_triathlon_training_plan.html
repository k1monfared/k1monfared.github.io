Training Intensity & Terminology Guide

Intensity Levels

Low Aerobic: Your natural "easy" pace in swimming, cycling or running.
Moderate Aerobic: An effort that is comfortable but not dawdling.
Threshold: An effort that is right on the threshold of making you really suffer.
Recovery: A very comfortable effort, deliberately slower than your natural pace.
VO2max: Maximal oxygen uptake ‚Äì exercise at or near maximum aerobic capacity (very high intensity, 90-100% effort).
Hard: An effort that is very challenging but not maximal for the prescribed duration.
Sprint: A 100 percent maximal effort.
Fartlek: Swedish for "speed play" ‚Äì alternating between faster and slower paces within the same workout.

Terminology

RI: Rest Interval ‚Äì a duration of time for recovery between intervals.
WU: Warm-up
MS: Main set
CD: Cooldown
