Further suggested reading:

  • Linear Algebra and its applications, David C. Lay, Addison-Wesley Publishing Company
    • Suitable for self study.
  • Vector Calculus, Linear Algebra, and Differential Forms, A Unified Approach, John H. Hubbard, Barbara Burke Hubbard, Prentice Hall
    • For readers with background in multivariable calculus.
  • Problems and Theorems in Linear Algebra (Translations of Mathematical Monographs, Vol. 134), V.V. Prasolov, American Mathematical Society
    • For readers with stronger background in mathematics and interested in various problems. A great source for some of the most famous and practical results in linear algebra.
  • Some study materials can be found on MIT-OCW's website: Link
